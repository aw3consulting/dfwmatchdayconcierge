# DFW Matchday Concierge — Target Authority

## Status

This file defines what the site is becoming.

It is a governing source-authority file and must be followed during implementation.

## Target Business Model

DFW Matchday Concierge is now a single-operator, single-vehicle private ride reservation and fan-support site.

The site is built around:

- One operator.
- One 2026 Black Tahoe.
- Premium private ride reservations.
- Dallas/Fort Worth match visitor support.
- Airport arrival and departure rides.
- Stadium pickup + return rides.
- FIFA Fan Festival / Fair Park pickup + return rides.
- Area watch-event rides.
- Fort Worth, Dallas, and Arlington experience routes.
- Limited availability.
- Direct reservation conversion.
- Next-leg conversion after the first ride.

## Primary Business Goal

The business goal is:

Capture the first ride, secure the return trip, and convert the guest into the next logical paid ride before the opportunity is missed.

The site must not behave like a generic service menu.

The site must behave like a reservation funnel that asks:

- Where are you now?
- Where are you going?
- How are you getting back?
- What next ride do you need while you are in DFW?
- Do you need your airport return before leaving?

## What This Site Is

The site is:

- A premium private SUV reservation site.
- A single-operator ride booking/fan support site.
- A direct lead-capture and reservation system.
- A Dallas/Fort Worth match visitor transportation support tool.
- A controlled platform for airport, stadium, Fan Festival, watch-event, and selected local experience rides.
- A booking funnel that prioritizes Pickup + Return.
- A field-validated operating tool.

## What This Site Is Not

The site is not:

- A redesign.
- A marketplace.
- A multi-driver platform.
- A generic tourism site.
- A general FIFA portal.
- A broad concierge marketplace.
- A team fan club site.
- An official FIFA service.
- An official stadium service.
- An official airport service.
- An official Fan Festival service.
- An official team transportation service.
- A sponsor-driven media site.

2026 Black Tahoe.

## Limited Availability Positioning

The site must state clearly:

This is a single-operator private SUV service with limited availability. Advance reservations are strongly encouraged, especially around matchdays, Fan Festival events, airport arrivals, and evening ride windows.

Suggested public language:

This is a single-operator private SUV service with limited availability. Reservations are encouraged at least 3 days in advance when possible. A ride request is not confirmed until accepted and confirmed directly.

## Alternate Driver Positioning

If the owner/operator is unavailable, the owner may attempt to connect the guest with an alternate driver using a similar-class vehicle.

This must be presented as possible but not guaranteed.

Approved language:

If I am unable to fulfill a requested ride, I may attempt to connect you with an alternate driver using a similar-class vehicle. Alternate driver availability, pricing, timing, and confirmation are not guaranteed.

Do not imply:

- Guaranteed alternate driver.
- Guaranteed same price.
- Guaranteed same vehicle.
- Guaranteed fulfillment.
- Fleet capacity.

## Priority Area Logic

Fort Worth is currently a proven high-profit lane and must be treated as a priority service area.

Dallas remains important, especially for restaurants, Fan Festival, airport, and stadium connections, but Dallas currently has conversion gaps that must be closed.

Arlington is a bridge market and should route guests to both Dallas and Fort Worth.

Frisco, Plano, and North Dallas are available by request but should remain secondary until demand is proven.

## Add-On Target Rule

Food, snacks, cooler, and concierge services are not standalone public service lanes.

They must be conditional deep-link add-ons that appear only after a primary ride context exists.

Add-ons must be logical for the route stage.

Do not offer generic add-ons that do not match the guest’s situation.

Example correction:

Do not lead airport-arrival users with cooler service.

Do lead airport-arrival users toward hotel drop-off, luggage support, future stadium ride, future Fan Festival ride, and airport return.

## Sponsor Target

Sponsors are allowed only as controlled, limited inventory.

Sponsors must not dominate the site, interrupt the booking flow, or appear above the primary Reserve a Ride CTA.

Sponsor placements must be clearly labeled.

Sponsor placement must not imply official FIFA, team, stadium, airport, venue, or event affiliation.
