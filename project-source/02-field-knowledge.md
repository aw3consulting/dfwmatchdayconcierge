# DFW Matchday Concierge — Field Knowledge Authority

## Status

This file captures owner-verified field observations from Dallas operations after the first week and after two matches.

This file is a governing source-authority file for site logic and booking-flow decisions.

## Field Knowledge Rule

These observations are business intelligence from the field.

They should guide the site’s conversion logic, page priority, route emphasis, and booking prompts.

They should not be copied into public site copy as raw operational commentary unless specifically approved.

## Overall Field Conclusion

The single-operator, single-vehicle model is more practical and more valuable than a broad fan concierge or marketplace build.

Visitors need simple, high-confidence private transportation help.

The strongest site logic is:

- Capture the first ride.
- Capture the return.
- Capture the next ride.
- Capture the airport return before the guest leaves.
- Use Dallas, Fort Worth, and Arlington routing to expand ride value.

## Fort Worth Field Knowledge

Fort Worth has been the most profitable lane so far.

Strong Fort Worth activity includes:

- Downtown Fort Worth to Stockyards.
- Stockyards to Downtown Fort Worth.
- Fort Worth hotel to stadium + return.
- Fort Worth guest to Dallas + return.
- Arlington to Fort Worth + return.
- Fort Worth restaurant / Stockyards / rodeo-related movement.

Fort Worth guests appear easier to convert into multiple-leg ride patterns because they move between:

- Downtown Fort Worth.
- Stockyards.
- Rodeo/nightlife.
- Arlington stadium area.
- Dallas restaurants or experiences.
- Airport return.

## Fort Worth Site Implication

Fort Worth must not be treated as secondary.

Fort Worth should be a priority conversion zone.

The site should encourage:

- Fort Worth local pickup + return.
- Stockyards pickup + return.
- Rodeo pickup + return.
- Fort Worth hotel to stadium + return.
- Fort Worth to Dallas + return.
- Fort Worth to airport return.

## Airport Field Knowledge

Observed successful pattern:

Airport pickup converted into stadium pickup + return, then should convert into airport return.

Current gap:

Airport return is not always being captured early enough.

## Airport Site Implication

Every airport arrival flow must ask about:

- Return airport ride.
- Stadium pickup + return.
- Fan Festival pickup + return.
- Dallas/Fort Worth experience rides during stay.
- Passenger count.
- Luggage count.
- Hotel/lodging area.
- Departure date/time if known.

The airport page should not treat arrival as a one-time ride.

Airport arrival is the beginning of a full-stay transportation relationship.

## Arlington Field Knowledge

Arlington is a bridge market.

Observed Arlington opportunity:

Arlington to Fort Worth.
Fort Worth restaurant / Terry Black’s Fort Worth if approved.
Stockyards.
Return to Arlington.

Observed additional pattern:

Arlington hotel to restaurant.
Restaurant to hotel.

Current gap:

Airport return was missed after a successful multi-leg ride.

## Arlington Site Implication

Arlington guests should be routed to both Dallas and Fort Worth.

Every Arlington flow should prompt:

- Stadium pickup + return.
- Fort Worth Stockyards / rodeo.
- Fort Worth restaurant route.
- Dallas restaurant route.
- Fan Festival / Fair Park.
- Airport return.

Arlington is not only a stadium area. It is the bridge point for both Dallas and Fort Worth ride expansion.

## Dallas Field Knowledge

Observed Dallas pattern:

Dallas hotel to Terry Black’s Dallas.
Terry Black’s Dallas to hotel.
Dallas Fan Festival drop-off.

Current Dallas gaps:

- Dallas restaurant rides are not yet converting strongly into stadium rides.
- Dallas restaurant rides are not yet converting strongly into Fort Worth rides.
- Dallas rides are not always converting into airport return.
- Fan Festival drop-offs are difficult to convert into return rides.

## Dallas Site Implication

Dallas flows must prompt next-leg conversion more clearly.

Dallas guests should be shown:

- Return to hotel.
- Stadium pickup + return.
- Fort Worth Stockyards / rodeo night.
- Fan Festival pickup + return.
- Airport return.
- Katy Trail Ice House.
- Terry Black’s Dallas.

Dallas guests should be subtly marketed Fort Worth attractions without depriving them of Dallas-area attractions.

## Fan Festival / Fair Park Field Knowledge

Fan Festival / Fair Park drop-off-only rides are difficult to convert into return rides.

The rider would need to be highly aware of the Fair Park area, entry/exit patterns, rideshare routing, and organizer traffic controls to self-manage a return ride well.

The area can be complicated for both guests and drivers.

## Fan Festival Site Implication

Fan Festival must be marketed as:

Pickup + Return.

Not as a simple drop-off.

The site should strongly encourage a scheduled return plan or flexible return window before the guest is dropped off.

Suggested public direction:

Fair Park event movement and post-event pickup can be complicated. Reserve your return plan in advance so you are not trying to arrange transportation after the event.

Do not promise exact pickup points if event routing may change.

Use controlled language:

Final pickup and return details are confirmed after reservation acceptance and may adjust based on current event traffic, closures, and access conditions.

## Stadium / Arlington Matchday Field Knowledge

Standard rideshare routing around the stadium has been inefficient and confusing.

Observed issues:

- Many riders are routed to a designated rideshare lot.
- Some riders may face long walks to Texas Live or the stadium.
- Traffic in and out is difficult.
- Post-match ride conditions are chaotic.
- Fans and drivers both experience confusion.
- Driver app behavior, data/network issues, designated lot confusion, rider signal issues, or dispatch behavior may all contribute to difficulty, but no public accusation should be made.

Observed private SUV opportunity:

- Pre-planned pickup + return.
- Known driver.
- Premium SUV.
- Hotel-area / Texas Live-area coordination when available.
- Better guest confidence than solving transportation after the match.

## Stadium Site Implication

The stadium page should sell:

- Stadium Pickup + Return.
- Pre-planned return.
- Texas Live / hotel-area planning when available.
- Avoiding post-match uncertainty.
- Private SUV ride planning.

The site must not promise illegal or restricted curb access.

The site must not promise exact stadium curbside pickup.

Use controlled public language:

Pickup and drop-off plans are confirmed after reservation acceptance and may adjust based on current matchday traffic controls, venue restrictions, and access conditions.

Keep this as owner-ops internal guidance.

## Frisco / Plano / North Dallas Field Knowledge

Frisco, Plano, and North Dallas may become useful lanes, but current field evidence shows most fans are looking to stay closer to the stadium.

The stronger current breakout is Arlington to Fort Worth and Dallas.

## Frisco / Plano / North Dallas Site Implication

Keep Frisco, Plano, and North Dallas available by request.

Do not prioritize these lanes over:

- Fort Worth.
- Arlington.
- Dallas.
- Airport.
- Stadium.
- Fan Festival.

Suggested positioning:

North Dallas / Plano / Frisco pickup may be available by request for airport transfers, stadium round trips, and Dallas/Fort Worth experience rides.
