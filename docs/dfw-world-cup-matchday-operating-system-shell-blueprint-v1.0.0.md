# DFW World Cup Matchday Operating System Shell Blueprint v1.0.0

## 1. Blueprint Purpose

This blueprint governs the rebuild of DFW Matchday Concierge into a DFW World Cup Matchday Operating System for arriving fans, international fans, media, corporate guests, groups, and event-support traffic.

The platform is not a generic transportation website and not a luxury landing page. It is a concierge booking platform, sports-event utility platform, tourist guidance layer, and matchday operations shell built to monetize intent while answering guest questions before they are asked.

## 2. Core Keyword: CONCIERGE

CONCIERGE means DFW Matchday Concierge accepts all inquiries within the matchday box, then routes each inquiry into one of four controlled outcomes:

1. Bookable service
2. Operator-assisted quote
3. Partner / driver / delivery assignment
4. Restricted or declined request

The public site should invite inquiry broadly while the backend and operator workflow control what is accepted, priced, assigned, restricted, or escalated.

Public posture:

`Tell us what you need for matchday. If it fits the Dallas/Fort Worth matchday box, we will help route it, quote it, or explain the next best option.`

## 11. Payment Workflow

Launch payment flow is operator-confirmed.

Required flow:

1. Guest submits inquiry.
2. Operator confirms price, availability, and service boundary.
3. Payment request or deposit request is sent.
4. Booking is confirmed only after payment or approved deposit.
5. Balance, add-ons, reimbursements, and custom purchases are settled before pickup, delivery, or completion.

### 11.1 Payment Methods

Potential methods:

- Zelle
- Cash App
- Venmo
- Square payment link
- Revolut payment request
- card payment link
- approved cash

Public claim rule:

`International payment options may be available by request.`

Do not claim full multi-currency support until tested end-to-end.

### 11.2 Deposit Logic

- Standard ride: deposit required to hold the time.
- Matchday package: larger deposit required.
- Food, drinks, cooler, essentials, OTC, and custom items: prepaid or deposit plus reimbursement rule.
- Large group / corporate / media route: quote required before confirmation.
- Multi-vehicle and recurring bookings: quote and deposit required.

### 11.3 Refund Policy Shell

Refunds depend on timing, assignment, purchased items, and confirmed expenses.

Recommended shell:

- Full refund before driver, delivery, or vendor assignment.
- Partial refund after driver, delivery, or vendor assignment.
- Purchased food, drinks, supplies, OTC items, ice, cooler items, and custom expenses are non-refundable once purchased.
- Missed pickup caused by guest delay after a grace period is not automatically refundable.
- Weather, event disruption, road closure, or official security disruption reviewed case by case.

## 15. Claim Gates

No public claim may appear unless mapped to one of these statuses:

- Live Capability
- Operator-Assisted Capability
- Development Capability
- Restricted Capability

### 15.1 Live / Operator-Assisted

Allowed when the operator can directly intake, quote, and fulfill or coordinate.

### 15.2 Development

Allowed only with future-state language, such as:

`Coming soon` or `Available by operator request while the automated workflow is in development.`

### 15.3 Restricted

Requires controlled language and disclaimers.

Restricted areas include:

- Ticket brokerage
- ticket marketplace
- ticket authentication guarantee
- official tournament affiliation
- escrow
- medical advice
- alcohol sale/service implication
- guaranteed driver supply
- guaranteed dispatch automation
- full multi-currency support without testing

## 16. Visual and UX Principles

The site must be highly visual, highly interactive, and simple.

Required UX principles:

- Team-first for fans
- Media/corporate lane first for commercial guests
- Booking widget above information
- Maps and routes as visual anchors
- Icons and cards to overcome language barriers
- Minimal text before first action
- Clear booking status
- Clear payment status
- Clear operator-confirmation requirement
- Upsells after intent

Avoid:

- generic luxury design
- white-glove cliches
- oversized meaningless headings
- black stealth aesthetic as the core experience
- fake automation
- unsupported capability claims
- information overload before action
