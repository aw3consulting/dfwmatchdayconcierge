# DFW VIP Concierge Operator Standard v1.0.0

## 1. Purpose

This file governs the human operating standard for DFW Matchday Concierge VIP Guest Concierge service, Team Family escalation, approved Media / Corporate escalation, Black 2026 Chevrolet Tahoe handling, privacy-sensitive guest support, credential-aware scheduling, itinerary control, and operator-confirmed fulfillment.

This standard defines how the primary VIP operator must behave before, during, and after an accepted VIP or VIP-adjacent assignment.

## 2. Core Determination

The VIP operator is not merely a driver.

The VIP operator functions as:

- concierge operator
- movement planner
- itinerary coordinator
- privacy-sensitive handler
- local DFW guide
- service quality controller
- problem solver
- fulfillment gatekeeper

Transportation is one function inside the operator standard. The broader duty is trip-value protection through controlled planning, communication, preparation, and execution.

## 5. Official VIP Vehicle Standard

Official VIP vehicle:

- 2026 Chevrolet Tahoe
- Black exterior
- Black interior
- Seats up to 7 passengers, subject to luggage and comfort review

The Tahoe is a VIP brand asset and must be presented only as available for approved VIP Guest Concierge, Team Family escalation, or approved high-priority assignments.

No public copy may claim guaranteed Tahoe availability before booking confirmation.

## 6. Operator Presentation Standard

Before each VIP or VIP-adjacent assignment, the operator must meet the following presentation standard:

- summer professional dress
- well groomed
- clean hygiene
- good health readiness
- respectful conduct
- courteous communication
- no alcohol
- no smoke
- no strong odors
- calm tone
- adaptable client service style

The operator must not appear rushed, informal, distracted, irritated, argumentative, or poorly prepared.

## 7. Vehicle Readiness Standard

Before each VIP assignment, the vehicle must meet the following readiness standard:

- daily detail or service-level cleaning
- disinfectant cleaning
- full tank of gas
- pre-service maintenance review
- interior comfort review
- luggage area review
- cooler / supply area review if applicable
- charging support reviewed if available
- climate comfort prepared

Internal checklist should confirm:

- exterior cleanliness
- interior cleanliness
- fuel level
- tire condition
- lights
- fluid concerns if visible
- cabin odor
- seat/cargo readiness
- route readiness

## 8. Communication Standard

Operator communication must be:

- clear
- calm
- concise
- respectful
- timely
- privacy-aware
- status-oriented
- solution-oriented

Avoid:

- casual oversharing
- sensitive speculation
- gossip
- celebrity/team discussion unless client initiates and it is appropriate
- public identity references
- unsupported promises
- arguing with client/coordinator/guest

Recommended communication pattern:

1. confirm receipt
2. confirm current status
3. identify next step
4. identify any missing requirement
5. provide realistic timing
6. avoid overpromising

## 9. Privacy and Anonymity Standard

VIP Guest Concierge and Team Family clients may request anonymity or privacy-sensitive handling.

Operator must:

- use first names, initials, booking codes, or coordinator names when appropriate
- limit identity exposure to need-to-know contexts
- avoid discussing client identity with third parties
- avoid posting or sharing client photos without written approval
- avoid public reference to client relationship, status, team, celebrity, or credential connection
- keep sensitive itinerary details controlled

Driver pool and fulfillment support receive only operationally necessary information.

## 10. Coordinator and Representative Handling

Many high-value clients may be represented by:

- executive assistant
- travel manager
- family coordinator
- media coordinator
- corporate coordinator
- sponsor / hospitality representative
- team or federation representative

Operator must distinguish:

- Hub Owner
- Principal Client
- Coordinator / Representative
- Guests
- Payment Authority
- Confirmation Authority

The operator must not assume the person communicating is the person receiving service.

## 11. Credential-Aware Scheduling Standard

Credential/access status may affect planning and priority, but DFW Matchday Concierge does not validate credentials or provide official access.

Allowed handling:

- accept client-provided access details for scheduling
- plan around credential-controlled locations when provided
- prioritize access-sensitive timing
- coordinate pickup/drop-off instructions based on client-provided access

Prohibited handling:

- claiming official credential validation
- claiming official tournament access
- claiming team/federation authorization
- guaranteeing access to restricted areas
- presenting the operator as officially credentialed unless independently confirmed and approved

## 12. Arrival Protocol

Arrival planning should consider:

- airport/FBO
- flight time
- immigration/customs uncertainty for international arrivals
- luggage volume
- guest count
- privacy preference
- hotel check-in timing
- first obligation timing
- food/water/comfort needs

Operator must confirm:

- pickup point
- contact method
- waiting/holding plan
- luggage expectations
- timing risks
- hotel or first-destination route

## 13. Stadium and Matchday Protocol

Matchday planning must consider:

- stadium arrival windows
- traffic buffers
- security/perimeter restrictions
- pickup/drop-off practicality
- post-match surge
- return timing
- food/water/cooler needs
- guest comfort
- credential/access details supplied by client

Operator must not guarantee exact arrival or pickup timing when stadium operations, traffic, law enforcement, event restrictions, or road closures may affect movement.

## 14. Food, Beverage, Cooler, and Comfort Support

Operator may coordinate:

- food pickup
- snacks
- water
- coffee
- drinks where lawful and controlled
- ice
- cups
- cooler setup
- comfort items

These are add-on or confirmed service elements, not automatic inclusions unless listed in the quote or confirmed itinerary.

Alcohol handling must remain lawful, controlled, and only offered when legally and operationally verified.

## 15. Luggage Coordination Standard

Luggage coordination must be reviewed against:

- passenger count
- bag count
- bag size
- vehicle assignment
- cooler/supply load
- comfort expectation
- itinerary timing
- security sensitivity

The public claim remains:

`Passenger and luggage capacity are confirmed during booking to preserve comfort and service quality.`

No exact luggage capacity should be promised until a passenger-to-luggage matrix is approved.

## 16. Security Coordination Standard

Security is currently an operator-assisted coordination possibility, not a guaranteed included service.

Allowed language:

`Security coordination may be available by request.`

Operator must not claim:

- guaranteed security
- armed security
- protective detail
- bodyguard service
- official security clearance

unless a verified provider, scope, legality, pricing, and confirmation process are approved.

## 17. Service Acceptance Standard

The operator may accept, waitlist, downgrade, refer, or decline service based on:

- capacity
- safety
- privacy risk
- payment readiness
- timing feasibility
- vehicle availability
- driver availability
- itinerary complexity
- legal or claim restrictions
- third-party provider feasibility

The operator must not accept service that creates an unsupported public claim or likely fulfillment failure.

## 18. Direct Fulfillment vs Referral Standard

When DFW Matchday Concierge cannot directly fulfill a service, operator may route to:

- third-party rideshare
- food delivery platform
- restaurant direct order
- attraction provider
- hotel concierge
- third-party transportation provider
- security provider by request if verified
- official source

Referral or affiliate status must be disclosed as appropriate.

The operator must distinguish direct service from third-party referral.

## 19. Issue Escalation Standard

Escalate when:

- client safety concern arises
- privacy breach risk exists
- schedule disruption affects core itinerary
- payment conflict arises
- route becomes infeasible
- third-party provider fails
- request becomes restricted or unlawful
- VIP/Team Family sensitivity increases

Escalation options:

- operator direct call
- coordinator call
- alternate route
- alternate provider
- waitlist
- downgrade
- refund/credit review
- decline/restrict

## 20. Service Closeout Standard

After service or planning completion, operator should review:

- completed itinerary items
- open add-ons
- payment/balance status
- client satisfaction
- referral/credit possibility
- follow-up opportunity
- future matchday needs
- incident notes if any

Closeout should preserve privacy and avoid public client reference unless approved.

## 22. Final Determination

The VIP Concierge Operator Standard is accepted as the human-service layer behind the DFW Matchday Concierge operating system.

The operator is governed as a concierge operator and movement planner, not merely a driver.

This standard protects VIP, Team Family, Media / Corporate escalation, Tahoe allocation, privacy-sensitive handling, operator-confirmed fulfillment, referral routing, and trip-value protection.
