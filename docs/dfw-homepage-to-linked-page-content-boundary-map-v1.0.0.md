## Purpose

This map defines what belongs on the homepage and what must move to linked pages.

It prevents the homepage from absorbing detailed content, repeating guide logic, replacing linked pages, or undermining sponsor monetization across category pages.

## Core Boundary Rule

The homepage introduces and routes.

Linked pages explain, compare, guide, source, hand off, and host page-level sponsorship.

A homepage concept may appear once as an introduction and once as a route. It may not be repeated as full content unless required by footer, navigation, accessibility, or legal disclaimer structure.

## Homepage Allowed Content Limit

The homepage may include no more than:

1. one hero welcome statement
2. one service summary section
3. one audience routing section
4. one sponsor opportunity section
5. one partner recognition section
6. one trust section
7. one footer and disclaimer

## Monetization Boundary

The homepage sells the idea of advertising.

The advertise page sells the packages.

The sponsors page recognizes approved partners.

Category pages host page-specific sponsor recognition and inventory.

The homepage must not list every sponsor category in detail because that reduces value from the sponsored linked pages.

## Failure Triggers

A homepage prototype fails if it:

1. repeats the same content area more than twice
2. explains detailed airport, transportation, dining, hotel, team, source, or provider content
3. turns into a guide index with long card copy
4. turns into a planning form page
5. turns into a sponsor inventory spreadsheet
6. replaces linked pages
7. hides sponsor opportunity
8. weakens advertiser value on linked pages
9. uses fake partner logos
10. implies official affiliation
