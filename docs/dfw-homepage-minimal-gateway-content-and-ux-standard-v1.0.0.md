# DFW Homepage Minimal Gateway Content and UX Standard v1.0.0

## Controlled Action

`dfw_homepage_minimal_gateway_content_and_ux_standard`

## Status

`LOCKED / MINIMAL GATEWAY STANDARD CREATED / HOMEPAGE PROTOTYPE REVISION BLOCKED UNTIL THIS STANDARD IS APPLIED / BATCH 1 REMAINS BLOCKED`

## Purpose

This standard defines what the DFW Matchday Information + Planning Hub homepage is allowed to be.

It exists because prior homepage recovery attempts correctly identified governance issues but still produced a homepage that absorbed too much detail from linked pages.

The homepage must now operate as a minimal premium gateway, not a directory, full guide, dashboard, internal command tool, or long planning article.

## Homepage Definition

The homepage is a public gateway that must:

1. introduce DFW Matchday Concierge
2. introduce the DFW Matchday Information + Planning Hub
3. welcome teams, supporters, fans, visitors, coordinators, local partners, and advertisers
4. qualify the platform as independent and unofficial
5. route visitors to the correct detailed pages
6. offer premium ad space and sponsor opportunities
7. recognize approved partners when partners exist
8. establish trust, responsibility, privacy, and claim safety

## Minimal Gateway Rule

The homepage may introduce a concept once and route to it once.

A concept must not appear more than twice on the homepage unless required by navigation, footer, legal, accessibility, or disclaimer structure.

The homepage must not explain every planning category in detail. Detailed content belongs on linked pages.

## Required Homepage Sections

The homepage must contain only the following primary sections:

1. Header and navigation
2. Hero and welcome
3. What DFW Matchday Concierge provides
4. Choose your path
5. Premium ad space and sponsorship
6. Partner recognition
7. Trust and responsibility
8. Footer and disclaimer

## Section Intent

### Header and navigation

The header must identify the brand, provide concise navigation, preserve sponsor or advertiser access, and avoid crowding.

### Hero and welcome

The hero must clearly introduce DFW Matchday Concierge and welcome the event audience while qualifying the platform as independent.

### What DFW Matchday Concierge provides

This section may summarize services and information pathways at a high level only.

Allowed high-level categories:

1. visitor information
2. matchday planning paths
3. airport and transportation guidance
4. hotels and area orientation
5. dining and watch-party routes
6. VIP, team, media, and corporate coordinator reviewed support
7. sponsor and partner visibility

### Choose your path

This section routes users to the correct page or pathway.

Allowed paths:

1. fans and supporters
2. families and groups
3. international visitors
4. VIP, team, media, and corporate coordinators
5. advertisers and local partners

### Premium ad space and sponsorship

This section must sell the commercial opportunity without listing every inventory detail.

It may include title sponsor availability, category sponsorship, guide page sponsorship, page-level ad space, and a call to view sponsor opportunities.

### Partner recognition

This section thanks partners and shows approved partner recognition only when sold and approved.

Until partners are approved, it must use available placement or partner space language only.

### Trust and responsibility

This section must establish independence, no official affiliation, source distinction, sponsor disclosure, privacy, and responsible planning language.

### Footer and disclaimer

The footer must keep legal and trust links concise.

## Forbidden Homepage Content

The homepage must not include:

1. full airport arrivals detail
2. full transportation detail
3. full dining or watch-party detail
4. full hotels and area detail
5. full team hub detail
6. full official-source explanation repeated across sections
7. full provider handoff explanation repeated across sections
8. dense planning forms that replace linked pages
9. long guide summaries
10. command dashboard behavior
11. content catalog behavior
12. internal operations language
13. general visitor Command Hub access
14. live dispatch, live booking, live payment, or live AI fulfillment claims unless validated and approved
15. fake sponsor logos
16. sold-looking sponsor treatment without approval
17. repeated concepts appearing more than twice without legal need

## Homepage Copy Standard

Homepage copy must be concise, scannable, and web-native.

Use:

1. short headings
2. short supporting copy
3. clear CTA labels
4. compact route cards
5. sponsor opportunity cards
6. trust statements

Avoid:

1. long explanatory paragraphs
2. repeated definitions
3. dense directories
4. duplicated category explanations
5. over-explaining the same platform role

## CTA Standard

Primary CTAs allowed:

1. Start Planning
2. View Guides
3. Advertise With Us

Secondary CTAs allowed:

1. Choose Your Path
2. View Sponsor Opportunities
3. Request Reviewed Support
4. View Partner Opportunities

Blocked primary CTAs:

1. Connect as a general visitor path
2. Book Now without validated booking capability
3. Dispatch Now without validated dispatch capability
4. Pay Now without validated payment capability
5. Command Hub for general visitors

## Sponsor Standard

The homepage may show sponsor opportunities, not full sponsor inventory detail.

Detailed sponsor inventory belongs on:

1. `advertise/index.html`
2. `sponsors/index.html`
3. relevant category guide pages

The homepage may display:

1. title sponsor available
2. guide page sponsor available
3. category sponsor available
4. partner recognition area
5. advertiser CTA

## Approval Rule

A future homepage prototype fails if it feels like any of the following:

1. planning directory
2. document
3. dashboard
4. internal command tool
5. content catalog
6. linked-page replacement
7. sponsor inventory spreadsheet
