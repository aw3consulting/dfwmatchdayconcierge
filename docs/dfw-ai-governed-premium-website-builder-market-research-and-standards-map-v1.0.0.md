## 8. DFW Relevance Determination

The market research confirms that the current website builder market strongly favors full-site generation, rapid landing-page assembly, template reuse, AI-assisted content, hosted platform convenience, and visual canvas editing. These strengths are also the primary failure risks for DFW Matchday Concierge because they can hide weak section decisions, insert unsupported claims, compress premium layout decisions, and generate whole-page artifacts before section-level approval.

The DFW builder module must therefore operate differently from market tools:

1. It must be repo-first, not platform-first.
2. It must be section-first, not homepage-first.
3. It must require section identity before content.
4. It must require option selection before skeleton rendering.
5. It must require CSS-rendered browser review before content insertion.
6. It must require desktop, mobile, accessibility, claim, and sponsor review before lock.
7. It must block final content insertion until user authorization.
8. It must treat market tools and AI tools as research and inspiration sources only.
