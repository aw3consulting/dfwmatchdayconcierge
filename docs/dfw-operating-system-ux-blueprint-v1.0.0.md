# DFW Operating System UX Blueprint v1.0.0

## 1. Purpose

This file governs the DFW Matchday Concierge user experience for the next visual mockup and frontend implementation pass.

The platform must now be designed as a DFW World Cup Matchday Operating System, not a transportation website.

This UX blueprint translates the completed commercial, AI intake, command hub, monetization, coordinator, VIP operator, secure access, and inquiry-to-confirmation governance into the public and private UX structure.

## 2. Core UX Determination

DFW Matchday Concierge is a matchday planning, concierge intake, command hub, referral, and optional fulfillment platform.

The UX must make this visible immediately.

The visitor is not simply booking a ride. The visitor is choosing a lane, building a matchday plan, verifying access, completing AI-assisted intake, previewing a command hub, activating planning, receiving quote review, paying a deposit or payment request, and moving into confirmed service or referral.

## 3. Governed Visitor Flow

The required flow is:

1. Public Site
2. Inquiry
3. Verification
4. AI Intake
5. Command Hub Preview
6. Activation
7. Itinerary
8. Quote
9. Payment / Deposit
10. Confirmation
11. Service or Referral

No UX state may visually imply confirmed service before operator approval, quote, payment/deposit status, and fulfillment path are complete.

## 4. Homepage Funnel

The homepage must be short, visual, action-led, and lane-led.

Required hierarchy:

1. Approved DFW Matchday Concierge logo
2. DFW World Cup Matchday Operating System positioning
3. One-sentence plain-language explanation
4. Four-lane selector
5. Fast inquiry action
6. DFW map/location preview
7. AI-assisted intake preview
8. Verified hub preview teaser
9. Public fan hub / free information teaser
10. Claim/status badge strip

Recommended homepage copy:

`Welcome to Dallas/Fort Worth for matchday. Choose your lane and build the command hub for your route, team, schedule, food, luggage, group, and concierge needs.`

## 5. Four-Lane Selector

The homepage must present four controlled lanes.

### 5.1 VIP Guest Concierge

Purpose:
Private, scarce-capacity, operator-approved concierge support.

Primary UX signals:

- VIP review by request
- Black 2026 Chevrolet Tahoe standard for approved VIP bookings
- privacy-sensitive handling
- itinerary planning
- food, cooler, luggage, and security coordination by request
- operator confirmation required

Public constraint:
No public guarantee of Tahoe availability, driver availability, security, official access, or VIP approval.

### 5.2 Team Family Support

Purpose:
Privacy-sensitive support for team-family, federation-family, staff-family, or representative-managed planning.

Primary UX signals:

- coordinator/representative support
- privacy-sensitive intake
- family/group itinerary support
- operator review required
- possible VIP escalation

Public constraint:
No public verification claim, official team relationship claim, credential validation claim, or guaranteed family access claim.

### 5.3 Media / Corporate

Purpose:
High-value company-card, group, media, sponsor, hospitality, production, and recurring movement lane.

Primary UX signals:

- organization-led intake
- recurring schedules
- airport, hotel, stadium, equipment, catering, and group support
- quote preparation
- payment pathway review
- operator-confirmed assignment

Public constraint:
No fleet guarantee, invoice guarantee, dispatch automation claim, or provider fulfillment guarantee.

### 5.4 Team Fan Hubs

Purpose:
Scalable public and activated fan planning.

Primary UX signals:

- choose team
- choose language
- route guidance
- Fan Experience planning
- shared ride request
- food, snack, cooler, and sightseeing options
- free hub plus optional activated planning

Public constraint:
No automatic shared ride match, direct fulfillment guarantee, VIP Tahoe access, official affiliation, or live data claim unless verified.

## 6. AI Inquiry Flow

AI intake must operate as a guide, not a booking agent.

AI may:

- ask structured intake questions
- identify missing details
- organize trip information
- suggest command hub widgets
- prepare an operator-review summary
- generate a preliminary hub preview

AI may not:

- confirm booking
- approve VIP status
- confirm Tahoe availability
- confirm driver assignment
- confirm security
- validate credentials
- set final pricing
- claim official event access
- collect raw card details

Required AI intake stages:

1. lane selection
2. coordinator/self identification
3. sensitive-status preference
4. arrival and departure dates
5. airport and hotel/housing details
6. matchday attendance
7. obligations and locations
8. transportation needs
9. food, drink, cooler, essentials, and luggage needs
10. security coordination interest
11. budget range and payment readiness
12. privacy and communication preference
13. submit for operator review

## 7. Verified Hub Preview

The verified hub preview is the conversion bridge between public browsing and activated planning.

Access requirement:

- email verification, mobile verification, secure link, or authenticated session

Preview may show:

- AI intake summary
- missing detail checklist
- recommended widgets
- preliminary route plan
- itinerary categories
- operator review required status
- activation CTA

Preview must not show:

- final price
- confirmed driver
- confirmed Tahoe
- confirmed security
- confirmed official access
- confirmed service
- payment-confirmed status

## 8. Activated Personal Matchday Command Hub

The activated hub is the primary personalized planning product.

Activated hub sections:

1. Client / coordinator profile
2. Guest details
3. Arrival plan
4. Departure plan
5. Hotel / housing plan
6. Matchday route plan
7. Itinerary builder
8. Food, drink, cooler, and essentials requests
9. Luggage coordination
10. Security coordination interest
11. VIP / Tahoe eligibility or assignment status
12. Quote status
13. Payment / deposit status
14. Booking status tracker
15. Operator-reviewed next steps
16. Referral/provider routing where direct fulfillment is unavailable

Activated hub does not equal confirmed service.

## 9. VIP Hub UX

The VIP hub must feel private, scarce, premium, and operator-reviewed.

Required UX elements:

- private header
- Black 2026 Chevrolet Tahoe profile, marked as subject to confirmation
- VIP operator profile, controlled and limited
- privacy-sensitive itinerary sections
- airport/FBO support area
- stadium/matchday movement area
- food, drink, cooler, luggage, and comfort request area
- security coordination request area
- quote and deposit status
- confirmed assignment status only after operator approval

Ad policy:
No ads inside VIP hubs unless explicitly approved as curated premium placement.

## 10. Team Family Hub UX

The Team Family hub must protect privacy and coordinator use.

Required UX elements:

- private family/support header
- representative/coordinator controls
- sensitive-status non-disclosure option
- family/group itinerary builder
- airport, hotel, stadium, food, luggage, and sightseeing planning
- VIP escalation review status
- operator review status
- payment authority status

Ad policy:
Ads prohibited.

## 11. Media / Corporate Hub UX

The Media / Corporate hub must prioritize quote-ready operations.

Required UX elements:

- organization/contact intake
- route and schedule builder
- passenger and equipment count
- vehicle count request
- recurring schedule request
- catering request
- credential timing notes supplied by client
- company-card or payment pathway status
- quote preparation state
- assignment status

Ad policy:
Ads prohibited inside private Media / Corporate hubs.

## 12. Team Fan Hub UX

Fan hubs must scale public traffic and preserve monetization without exhausting operator capacity.

Required UX elements:

- team selector
- language selector
- match card
- free guidance layer
- route guidance
- Fan Experience guidance
- food and attraction recommendations
- shared ride request CTA
- activated planning CTA
- referral/affiliate/API zones where approved
- ad zones where appropriate

Fan hub public CTA:

`Start with free matchday guidance. Activate planning when you want a personalized itinerary, operator review, and quote-ready concierge support.`

The map/location layer must help users understand DFW geography quickly.

Required map points:

- DFW Airport
- Dallas Love Field
- Dallas
- Arlington Stadium
- Fort Worth
- Fort Worth Stockyards
- FIFA Fan Experience
- hotel area placeholder
- user-entered location placeholder

Required route lines:

- DFW Airport to Arlington
- Dallas Love Field to Dallas
- Dallas to Arlington
- Fort Worth to Arlington
- Arlington to Fan Experience
- Dallas to Stockyards
- hotel to stadium

Map rule:

No map may claim live routing, live traffic, or real-time dispatch unless implemented and tested.

## 14. Monetization-Safe Zones

### 14.1 Allowed ad/referral zones

Allowed when controlled:

- public homepage information layer
- public Team Fan Hubs
- free fan guidance pages
- non-paying fan previews
- attraction recommendation panels
- restaurant/provider recommendation panels
- official-source guidance panels
- third-party referral areas

### 14.2 Restricted ad/referral zones

Ads should not appear in:

- VIP activated hubs
- Team Family hubs
- private Media / Corporate hubs
- privacy-sensitive hubs
- paid premium command hubs

unless explicitly approved as curated premium placement.

### 14.3 Required disclosure

The UX must distinguish:

- direct DFW service
- operator-assisted coordination
- third-party referral
- affiliate/referral link
- API-integrated provider
- restricted or unavailable service

## 15. Claim and Status Badges

Every public claim must map to one badge.

Allowed badge set:

- Live Capability
- Operator-Assisted
- Development
- Restricted
- Referral / Third-Party
- Information Only
- Planning Value
- Payment Pending
- Operator Review Required
- Confirmed

No badge may imply direct fulfillment before it is confirmed.
